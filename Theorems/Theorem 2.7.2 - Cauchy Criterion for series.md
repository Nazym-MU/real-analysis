The series $\sum^\infty_{k=1} a_k$ converges if and only if, given $\epsilon > 0, \ \exists \ N \in \mathbb N$ s.t. whenever $n > m \geq N$, it follows that $$|a_{m+1} + a_{m+2} + \dots + a_n| < \epsilon$$
**Proof:**
Observe that $$|s_n - s_m| = |a_{m+1} + a_{m+2} + \dots + a_n|$$ and apply [[Theorem 2.6.4 - Cauchy Criterion]].
