If $\sum_{k=1}^\infty a_k = A$ and $\sum_{k=1}^\infty b_k = B$, then
1. $\sum_{k=1}^\infty ca_k = cA \ \forall \ c \in \mathbb R$
2. $\sum_{k=1}^\infty (a_k + b_k) = A + B$

**Proof for 1:**
Given: $(a_k) \to A$, meaning $s_m = a_1 + a_2 + \dots + a_m$ converges to $A$.
WTS: $t_m = ca_1 + ca_2 + \dots + ca_m$ converges to $cA$. 

Since $t_m = cs_m$, applying [[Theorem 2.3.3 - Algebraic Limit Theorem]] yields $(t_m) \to cA. \qquad \square$


Infinite addition still satisfies the distributive property, and series can be added in the usual way.
