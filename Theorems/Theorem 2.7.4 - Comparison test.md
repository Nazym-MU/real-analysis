Assume $(a_k)$ and $(b_k)$ are sequences satisfying $0 \leq a_k \leq b_k \ \forall \ k \in \mathbb N$.
1. If $\sum^\infty_{k=1}b_k$ converges, then $\sum^\infty_{k=1}a_k$ converges.
2. If $\sum^\infty_{k=1}a_k$ diverges, then $\sum^\infty_{k=1}b_k$ diverges.

**Proof:**
Follow [[Theorem 2.7.2 - Cauchy Criterion for series]]. Observe that $$|a_{m+1} + a_{m+2} + \cdots + a_n| \leq |b_{m+1} + b_{m+2} + \cdots + b_n|. \qquad \square$$
