Cauchy sequences are bounded.
**Proof:**
Given $\epsilon = 1$, $\exists \ N\in \mathbb N$ s.t. $|x_m - x_n| < 1 \ \forall \ m, n \geq N$. 
Thus, we must have $|x_n| < |x_N| + 1 \ \forall \ n\geq N$. It follows that $$M = \max \{|x_1|,|x_2|, |x_3|,...,|x_{N-1}|, |x_N| + 1 \}$$ is a bound for the sequence $(x_n)$.
