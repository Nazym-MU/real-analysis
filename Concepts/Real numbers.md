We know that: $$N \subseteq Z \subseteq  Q$$
where $N$ - natural numbers, $Z$ - integers, $Q$ - rational numbers
Real numbers $R$ are the union of irrational and rational numbers

Properties:
- Set containing $\mathbb{Q}$
- Every element of $\mathbb{R}$ has an additive inverse, and every nonzero element has a multiplicative inverse
- It's a [[Field|field]]
- Ordering applies (it's an ordered field)
- [[Axiom of completeness]]
