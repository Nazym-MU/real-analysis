A set $O \subseteq \mathbb R$ is open if for all points $a \in O$, there exists an $\epsilon$-neighborhood $V_\epsilon(a)\subseteq O$.
Examples:
- $\mathbb R$, considering $\emptyset$ as an open subset of the real line
- $(c, d) = \{ x\in \mathbb R: c < x < d \}$
- The union of two open intervals is open
