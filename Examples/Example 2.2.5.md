We claim that$$\lim \left( \frac{1}{\sqrt n} \right) = 0$$
**Proof:**
Let $\epsilon > 0$ be an arbitrary positive number. Choose a natural number $N$ satisfying $$N > \frac{1}{\epsilon^2}$$
We now verify that this choice of $N$ has the desired property. Let $n \geq N$. Then, 
$$n > \frac{1}{\epsilon^2} \quad \text{implies} \quad \frac{1}{\sqrt n} < \epsilon \quad \text{and hence} \quad |a_n - 0| < \epsilon. \qquad \square$$
